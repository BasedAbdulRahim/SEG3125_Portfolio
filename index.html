About Me

How I Work

As a student currently enrolled in SEG3125 – Human-Computer Interaction at the University of Ottawa, I’m actively learning core UI/UX concepts like usability heuristics, accessibility, user-centered design, and prototyping techniques. My design process is rooted in iteration — from early sketching to responsive front-end development using tools like Bootstrap 5, JavaScript, and HTML/CSS.

For this portfolio, I'm applying key principles of visual communication such as visual hierarchy, balance, and contrast — themes covered in our course readings and NN/g lectures. This page reflects not only my current skills but also my desire to grow as a thoughtful, user-first designer. As the semester progresses, I plan to document each of my projects with insights into my workflow, design decisions, and lessons learned.

For external resources that influence my process, I often reference the Nielsen Norman Group, Interaction Design Foundation, and design communities such as Dribbble and Behance.
